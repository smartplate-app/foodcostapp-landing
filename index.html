Food Cost App — Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about managing food cost for your restaurant.

What is a food cost app?

A food cost app helps restaurant owners and managers track the cost of ingredients and supplier invoices, calculate food cost percentage, and monitor how it compares to their sales — all in real time. FoodCostApp automates this process so you always know where your money is going.

How do I calculate food cost percentage?

Food cost percentage = (Total food cost ÷ Total food sales) × 100. For example, if you spent ₪30,000 on food and made ₪100,000 in food sales, your food cost is 30%. A healthy target for most restaurants is between 28%–35%. FoodCostApp calculates this automatically using your invoices and POS data.

Is FoodCostApp a MarketMan alternative?

Yes. FoodCostApp provides the same core functionality as MarketMan — invoice scanning, supplier management, food cost tracking, and multi-location support — but with Hebrew/RTL support, local Israeli POS integrations (Tabit, BeeComm), and at a significantly lower price point. No setup fees, no long contracts.

Does FoodCostApp work in Hebrew?

Absolutely. FoodCostApp was built with Israeli restaurants in mind. The interface is fully available in Hebrew with RTL (right-to-left) layout, and our support team speaks Hebrew.

What POS systems does FoodCostApp integrate with?

FoodCostApp integrates with Tabit, BeeComm/BeePort, and other major Israeli POS systems. This allows automatic syncing of daily sales data so your food cost percentage is always up to date without any manual input.

How long does setup take?

Most restaurants are fully set up within 10–15 minutes. You add your location, connect your POS (optional), and start scanning invoices. There's no lengthy onboarding or training required.

Is there a free trial?

Yes. FoodCostApp offers a free trial with no credit card required. You can scan invoices, set up suppliers, and test the dashboard before committing to any plan.

Can I manage multiple restaurant locations?

Yes. FoodCostApp supports multi-location management. You can view all your branches from a single account, compare food cost performance, and manage suppliers across locations.
